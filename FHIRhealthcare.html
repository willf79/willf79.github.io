Electronic Medical Records to FHIR Standardization and Patient Journey Timeline

Cambridge Semantics Intern Project

FHIR: "a standard for health care data exchange, published by HL7" (FHIR v4.3.0).

Project Plan

FHIR AnzoGraph Model

A graphical representation of the FHIR standardized model that needed to be applied to patient records. There was an excess of potential fields within the standardization that needed to be sifted through; desired fields, common across patient records across hospital groups, were marked.

Patient Journey Timeline

The second main objective of the project, a timeline with nodes that represent each patient's interactions with a given hospital group. The data associated with the patient is standardizes to FHIR.

A singular patient's journey timeline. In a real-world application, a healthcare professional would be able to find their patient within table and then drill-down into their individual timeline. Then, the invidual interactions can be drilled-down into as well, allowing the healthcare professional quick and easy access to a patient's stored information, such as doctor's notes or X-rays.

Linking Overview

A simple canonical model intended to show how CSV files of synthetic patient records ("generated by SyntheaTM, an open-source patient population simulation made available by The MITRE Corporation") will interact and merge with the FHIR standardization. The patient records and FHIR standardization will both be children under a patient parent class. The parent class will allow data to be pulled from the patient records with the FHIR standardization acting similar to a filter.
